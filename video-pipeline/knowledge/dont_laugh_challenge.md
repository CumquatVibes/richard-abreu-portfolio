## FORMAT OVERVIEW

"Try Not To Laugh" / "Don't Laugh Challenge" is a compilation format where:
- Funny clips are played back-to-back
- Optional on-screen reactions, commentary, or timer overlays
- Engagement driven by the "challenge" framing
- Massive view potential (100M+ views common on top videos)

---

## COPYRIGHT & MONETIZATION RISKS

### High Risk:
- Using other people's clips WITHOUT permission = copyright strikes
- Pure compilation of others' clips = "reused content" → demonetization
- YouTube Content ID can auto-detect clips from popular sources

### How to Stay Monetized:
1. **Use ORIGINAL funny content** — not clips from other creators
2. **Add meaningful commentary** — voiceover reactions, text overlays, editorial framing
3. **Transform the content** — edits, sound effects, pacing changes, graphics
4. **Use licensed/CC content** — clips with explicit permission
5. **Create AI-generated funny scenarios** — fully original, no reuse risk

### YouTube Policy (July 2025):
- "Inauthentic content" policy covers compilations
- Must demonstrate "transformative delta" — clear human creative input
- Reused content evaluated CHANNEL-WIDE — one bad video can kill monetization

## PRODUCTION PIPELINE

### For RichComedy Channel:
1. **Script**: Generate list of 15-20 funny scenarios/prompts
2. **B-roll**: AI-generate comedic images for each scenario
3. **Voiceover**: Richard's voice narrating, reacting, timing the challenge
4. **Assembly**: Fast-paced editing with:
   - Timer overlay ("Don't laugh for 60 seconds!")
   - Sound effects (boing, crash, record scratch)
   - Score counter
   - Text overlays with punchlines
5. **Duration**: 10-15 minutes (optimal for mid-roll ads)
6. **Shorts**: Extract best 3-5 moments as Shorts

### Editing Style (from research):
- **Jump cuts** — fast-paced, TikTok-influenced
- **Sound effects** — sparingly, timed to punchlines
- **Music** — fast beats complement rapid cuts, pauses enhance timing
- **Exaggerated SFX** — "boing" for funny jumps, crash for slapstick
- **Pacing** — build tension before punchline, then quick release
