## Chapter 2: Recipe 1: Quinoa & Chickpea Salad with Lemon-Herb Vinaigrette (1:30-3:00)

First up, we have a vibrant Quinoa & Chickpea Salad packed with plant-based protein. This one is perfect for vegetarians and vegans, or anyone looking to lighten things up.

**Ingredients:**

*   1 cup cooked quinoa (about 8g protein per cup)
*   1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained (about 39g protein per can)
*   1/2 cup chopped cucumber
*   1/2 cup chopped red bell pepper
*   1/4 cup chopped red onion
*   1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
*   2 tablespoons olive oil
*   2 tablespoons lemon juice
*   1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
*   1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
*   Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1.  Combine the cooked quinoa, chickpeas, cucumber, red bell pepper, red onion, and parsley in a large bowl.
2.  In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
3.  Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
4.  Divide into meal prep containers.

[VISUAL: Step-by-step visuals showing the preparation of the salad. Emphasize the vibrant colors of the ingredients. Close-up shots of the dressing being whisked. Final shot of the salad being divided into meal prep containers.]

This salad is bursting with flavor and clocks in at approximately 15g of protein per serving, making it a great way to kick off our meal prep challenge!

## Chapter 3: Recipe 2: Turkey & Avocado Lettuce Wraps (3:00-4:30)

Next up, we're ditching the bread with these delicious Turkey & Avocado Lettuce Wraps. This is a low-carb option that's packed with protein and healthy fats.

**Ingredients:**

*   1 pound ground turkey (about 28g protein per 4oz)
*   1/2 cup chopped onion
*   1 clove garlic, minced
*   1/4 cup soy sauce (low sodium is recommended)
*   1 tablespoon honey
*   1 teaspoon sesame oil
*   1 head of butter lettuce, leaves separated
*   1 avocado, sliced
*   Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

**Instructions:**

1.  Cook the ground turkey in a skillet over medium heat until browned. Drain off any excess grease.
2.  Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until softened.
3.  Stir in the soy sauce, honey, and sesame oil. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce has thickened slightly.
4.  Spoon the turkey mixture into the lettuce leaves.
5.  Top with sliced avocado and sesame seeds (optional).
6.  Divide into meal prep containers, keeping the lettuce separate if possible to prevent wilting.

[VISUAL: Show the ground turkey being cooked and browned. Close-up shots of the sauce simmering. Demonstrate how to assemble the lettuce wraps. Show the finished wraps being placed in meal prep containers, with a tip on how to keep the lettuce fresh.]

These wraps are not only delicious but also super quick to prepare and offer around 25g of protein per serving!

## Chapter 4: Recipe 3: Tuna Salad with Greek Yogurt (4:30-6:00)

Let's keep things classic with a healthy twist! This Tuna Salad with Greek Yogurt is a lighter, higher-protein alternative to traditional mayonnaise-based tuna salad.

**Ingredients:**

*   2 cans (5 oz each) tuna in water, drained (about 40g protein per can)
*   1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt (about 20g protein per cup)
*   1/4 cup chopped celery
*   1/4 cup chopped red onion
*   2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
*   1 tablespoon lemon juice
*   Salt and pepper to taste
*   Whole wheat crackers or lettuce cups for serving

**Instructions:**

1.  In a medium bowl, combine the drained tuna, Greek yogurt, celery, red onion, dill, and lemon juice.
2.  Season with salt and pepper to taste.
3.  Mix well.
4.  Divide into meal prep containers and serve with whole wheat crackers or lettuce cups.

[VISUAL: Show the tuna being drained. Demonstrate how to mix the ingredients together. Focus on the creamy texture of the Greek yogurt. Show the finished tuna salad being served with both crackers and lettuce cups.]

This recipe is a protein powerhouse, providing around 30g of protein per serving, and it takes less than 10 minutes to make!

## Chapter 5: Recipe 4: Egg & Black Bean Burrito Bowls (6:00-7:30)

These Egg & Black Bean Burrito Bowls are a hearty and flavorful option that will keep you full for hours.

**Ingredients:**

*   8 large eggs (about 6g protein per egg)
*   1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained (about 39g protein per can)
*   1/2 cup cooked brown rice
*   1/2 cup salsa
*   1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)
*   1/4 cup chopped cilantro
*   Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1.  Scramble the eggs in a skillet over medium heat until cooked through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
2.  Divide the cooked brown rice, black beans, and scrambled eggs into meal prep containers.
3.  Top with salsa, shredded cheddar cheese (optional), and chopped cilantro.

[VISUAL: Show the eggs being scrambled. Highlight the vibrant colors of the salsa. Demonstrate how to assemble the burrito bowls. Show the finished bowls with and without cheese.]

Each bowl packs around 25g of protein and is packed with fiber and other essential nutrients.

## Chapter 6: Recipe 5: Chicken & Vegetable Skewers with Peanut Sauce (7:30-9:00)

Finally, we have these fun and flavorful Chicken & Vegetable Skewers with Peanut Sauce. This is a great way to sneak in extra veggies and keep things interesting.

**Ingredients:**

*   1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch cubes (about 26g protein per 4oz)
*   1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
*   1 yellow bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
*   1 zucchini, cut into 1-inch pieces
*   1 red onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
*   Wooden skewers

**Peanut Sauce:**

*   1/4 cup peanut butter
*   2 tablespoons soy sauce (low sodium recommended)
*   1 tablespoon honey
*   1 tablespoon rice vinegar
*   1 tablespoon sesame oil
*   1 tablespoon water
*   1/2 teaspoon ginger, grated
*   1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

**Instructions:**

1.  Thread the chicken and vegetables onto the wooden skewers.
2.  Grill or bake the skewers until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
3.  While the skewers are cooking, prepare the peanut sauce by whisking together all the ingredients in a small bowl.
4.  Drizzle the peanut sauce over the skewers.
5.  Divide into meal prep containers.

These skewers are packed with flavor and provide around 30g of protein per serving, making them a satisfying and healthy lunch option.

## Chapter 7: Meal Prep Tips and Storage (9:00-10:30)

Alright, now that we've whipped up these delicious lunches, let's talk about some meal prep tips and proper storage to ensure your food stays fresh and safe throughout the week.

[VISUAL: Show various types of meal prep containers – glass, plastic, divided. Labeling machine. Refrigerator organization.]

*   **Invest in good quality containers:** Opt for airtight containers to keep your food fresh and prevent leaks. Glass containers are great for reheating and avoiding plastic leaching.
*   **Cool food completely before storing:** This prevents condensation and bacteria growth.
*   **Label everything:** Include the date and contents for easy identification.
*   **Store properly:** Keep your lunches refrigerated at 40°F (4°C) or below. The FDA recommends consuming cooked foods within 3-4 days for optimal freshness and safety.
*   **Don't be afraid to freeze:** Some of these lunches, like the burrito bowls, can be frozen for longer storage. Just be sure to thaw them completely before reheating.

[VISUAL: On-screen text highlighting these tips: "Airtight Containers," "Cool Before Storing," "Label Everything," "Refrigerate Properly," "Freezing Tips."]

By following these simple tips, you can ensure your meal prepped lunches stay delicious and safe all week long!
