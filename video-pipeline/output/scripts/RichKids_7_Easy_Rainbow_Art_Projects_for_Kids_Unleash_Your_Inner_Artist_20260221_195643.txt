## Chapter 2: Rainbow Rice Art (1:00-2:30)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: rice, food coloring, vinegar, ziplock bags, glue, paper.]

Our first project is Rainbow Rice Art! This is a great sensory experience and it looks amazing when it's done.

Here's what you'll need: plain white rice, food coloring (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple!), white vinegar, ziplock bags, white glue, and a piece of paper or cardboard.

First, divide the rice into six ziplock bags – one for each color of the rainbow. In each bag, add a few drops of food coloring and about a teaspoon of white vinegar. The vinegar helps the color stick to the rice.

[VISUAL: Close-up of the process of adding food coloring and vinegar to the rice in the bag.]

Seal the bags tightly and shake them until the rice is completely coated in color. If the color isn't dark enough, add a few more drops of food coloring.

Once the rice is colored, spread it out on a plate to dry for at least an hour. This is important so the rice doesn’t clump together when you glue it.

[VISUAL: Time-lapse of the rice drying.]

Now for the fun part! Draw a rainbow outline on your paper or cardboard. Apply glue to one section of the rainbow at a time and sprinkle the corresponding color of rice onto the glue. Gently press the rice down.

[VISUAL: Child carefully applying glue and sprinkling colored rice onto the rainbow outline.]

Repeat this process for each color of the rainbow. Let the glue dry completely and then gently shake off any excess rice. You now have a beautiful, textured rainbow rice masterpiece!

## Chapter 3: Rainbow Paper Plate Weaving (2:30-4:00)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: paper plate, scissors, colorful yarn.]

Next up, we're making a Rainbow Paper Plate Weaving! This project is great for developing fine motor skills and creating a unique piece of art.

You'll need: a paper plate, scissors, and colorful yarn (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple!).

First, fold the paper plate in half and make an even number of cuts along the folded edge, stopping about an inch from the rim. These cuts will create the slots for your weaving.

[VISUAL: Close-up of the process of cutting the slits into the paper plate.]

Next, take your first color of yarn and tape one end to the back of the plate. Now, weave the yarn over and under the strips of paper.

[VISUAL: Child weaving yarn over and under the paper plate strips.]

When you reach the end, wrap the yarn around the last strip and continue weaving in the opposite direction. Change to a new color when you want and repeat the weaving process until the entire plate is filled.

Once you're done weaving, tie off the yarn and tape the ends to the back of the plate. You can even add a hanging loop to display your colorful woven rainbow!

## Chapter 4: Rainbow Handprint Art (4:00-5:30)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: washable paint (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple), paper, paper towels.]

Get ready to get a little messy with Rainbow Handprint Art! This is a classic and super fun project that kids love.

You'll need: washable paint in all the colors of the rainbow, a large piece of paper, and paper towels.

Start by squeezing a small amount of each color of paint onto a plate or palette. One color for each row of the rainbow.

[VISUAL: Close-up of the paint being squeezed onto the plate.]

Dip your hand into the red paint and make a handprint at the top of the paper. Clean your hand thoroughly with a paper towel before moving on to the next color.

[VISUAL: Child dipping their hand into the paint and making a handprint on the paper. Emphasis on cleaning hands between colors.]

Repeat this process for each color of the rainbow, working your way down the paper. Make sure each handprint overlaps slightly to create a connected rainbow.

Let the paint dry completely and you have a beautiful and personalized rainbow handprint masterpiece!

## Chapter 5: Crayon Resist Rainbow Painting (5:30-7:00)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: white crayon, watercolor paints, paintbrush, paper.]

Our next project is a bit of a magic trick! We're making a Crayon Resist Rainbow Painting.

You'll need: a white crayon, watercolor paints, a paintbrush, and paper.

Before you start painting, draw a rainbow on your paper using the white crayon. Since it's white on white, you won't be able to see it, but trust us, it's there!

[VISUAL: Close-up of the white crayon being used to draw a rainbow on the paper.]

Now, using your watercolor paints, paint over the entire paper with different colors. As you paint, you'll see the crayon rainbow magically appear!

[VISUAL: Time-lapse of the watercolor paints being applied to the paper, revealing the crayon rainbow.]

The wax from the crayon resists the watercolor paint, creating a beautiful and unique rainbow effect. This is a great way to teach kids about the properties of different materials.

## Chapter 6: Rainbow Pasta Necklace (7:00-8:30)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: uncooked pasta (penne or macaroni), food coloring, rubbing alcohol, ziplock bags, string or yarn.]

Let’s get crafty with Rainbow Pasta Necklaces! It's wearable art!

You'll need: uncooked pasta (penne or macaroni work best), food coloring, rubbing alcohol, ziplock bags, and string or yarn.

Just like with the rice art, divide the pasta into six ziplock bags. In each bag, add a few drops of food coloring and a tablespoon of rubbing alcohol. The rubbing alcohol helps the color distribute evenly and dries quickly.

[VISUAL: Close-up of the process of adding food coloring and rubbing alcohol to the pasta in the bag.]

Seal the bags tightly and shake until the pasta is completely coated. Spread the pasta out on a paper towel to dry completely.

[VISUAL: Time-lapse of the pasta drying.]

Once the pasta is dry, string it onto your yarn or string in rainbow order. Tie the ends together and you have a stylish and colorful rainbow pasta necklace!

[VISUAL: Child proudly wearing their rainbow pasta necklace.]

## Chapter 7: Rainbow Streamer Mobile (8:30-10:00)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: embroidery hoop, colorful streamers (paper or fabric), string, scissors.]

Time to bring some movement to our art with a Rainbow Streamer Mobile! This project will brighten up any room.

You'll need: an embroidery hoop, colorful streamers (paper or fabric), string, and scissors.

First, cut your streamers into different lengths, making sure you have all the colors of the rainbow.

[VISUAL: Close-up of cutting streamers into different lengths.]

Tie the streamers to the embroidery hoop, spacing them evenly around the hoop. You can experiment with different lengths and arrangements to create a unique design.

[VISUAL: Child tying the streamers onto the embroidery hoop.]

Once all the streamers are attached, cut three pieces of string of equal length. Tie each piece of string to the embroidery hoop at equally spaced points. Gather the loose ends of the string and tie them together to create a hanging loop.

Hang your rainbow streamer mobile and watch it dance in the breeze!

## Chapter 8: Rainbow Rock Painting (10:00-11:30)

[VISUAL: Overhead shot of the ingredients and materials: smooth rocks, acrylic paints (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple), paintbrushes, sealant (optional).]

Our final project is Rainbow Rock Painting! It's a fun way to decorate your garden or share art with your neighborhood.

You’ll need: smooth rocks, acrylic paints in rainbow colors, paintbrushes, and sealant (optional but recommended for outdoor use).

Start by cleaning your rocks and letting them dry completely. Then, using your acrylic paints, paint a rainbow design on each rock. You can paint stripes, swirls, or any other rainbow pattern you like.

[VISUAL: Close-up of different rainbow designs being painted on rocks.]

Let the paint dry completely, and then apply a coat of sealant to protect the paint if you plan to display your rocks outdoors. Now you can hide your rainbow rocks around your neighborhood for others to find and enjoy, or decorate your own garden with these colorful creations! Rock painting is a growing trend, with many communities participating in "rock hiding" events.

## Chapter 9: The Science of Rainbows (11:30 - 12:30)

[VISUAL: Animated diagram showing how rainbows are formed through light refraction and reflection in raindrops.]

Did you know that rainbows aren't actually objects? They're optical phenomena caused by refraction and reflection of light in water droplets. Sunlight enters a raindrop, slows down and bends (refracts). The light then reflects off the back of the raindrop and exits, refracting again as it leaves. This separates the white light into its constituent colors – red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. The order of the colors is always the same because each color bends at a slightly different angle. That’s why rainbows always appear in the same order. Pretty cool, right?
