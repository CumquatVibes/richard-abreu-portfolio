## Chapter 3: Recession-Proof Hustle #1: Online Tutoring (2:30-3:30)

[VOICEOVER] First on our list is online tutoring. Even during a recession, people will continue to invest in education, especially for their children. Platforms like Chegg, TutorMe, and Skooli are always in demand, and you can tutor in subjects you're already knowledgeable in – math, science, English, even music or coding. The earning potential is significant, with many tutors earning $20-$50 per hour. You'll need a reliable internet connection, a webcam, and a passion for helping others. Focus on subjects that are highly sought after like math, science and test prep like the SAT and ACT.

## Chapter 4: Recession-Proof Hustle #2: Virtual Assistant Services (3:30-4:30)

[VOICEOVER] Next up is providing virtual assistant services. Businesses, especially small businesses and entrepreneurs, are always looking for help with administrative tasks, social media management, and customer service. Even during a recession, they need to stay efficient and competitive. Platforms like Upwork and Fiverr are great places to find clients, and you can offer a wide range of services based on your skillset. Rates vary depending on the services you offer, but experienced virtual assistants can easily earn $25-$75 per hour. Focus on offering services that are truly needed by businesses during a slow down - like cost cutting and marketing!

[VOICEOVER] Content is king, and businesses will always need compelling content, even during a recession. ## Chapter 6: Recession-Proof Hustle #4: E-commerce and Print-on-Demand (5:30-6:30)

[VOICEOVER] E-commerce isn't going anywhere, even during a recession. But instead of investing heavily in inventory, consider print-on-demand. With services like Printful and Printify, you can design and sell t-shirts, mugs, posters, and other products without holding any inventory yourself. When a customer places an order, the print-on-demand service handles the printing, packaging, and shipping. Your focus is on creating compelling designs and marketing your products. Platforms like Etsy and Shopify make it easy to set up your own online store. Focus on recession-related or humorous designs that resonate with people during difficult times.

## Chapter 7: Recession-Proof Hustle #5: Repair Services (6:30-7:30)

[VOICEOVER] When money is tight, people are more likely to repair items rather than replace them. That makes repair services a solid recession-proof hustle. If you have skills in electronics repair, appliance repair, or even bicycle repair, you can offer your services to your local community. Platforms like Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace are great places to advertise, and word-of-mouth referrals can be powerful. Basic tools and skills are required, but the earning potential can be substantial, especially if you specialize in a high-demand area. Advertise your services with savings - help people save money on replacing items and save the environment at the same time!
